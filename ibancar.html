CASE STUDY 02

Redesigning the Loan Application Experience

How I increased conversion rates by simplifying a complex financial onboarding flow for Ibancar

Role

Lead UX/UI Designer

Timeline

10 months

Team

3 Developers, Marketing, CEO

Tools

Figma, Microsoft Clarity, Google Analytics, Useberry, Jira

About Ibancar

Ibancar is a car-secured loan platform serving clients across Spain. The company connects investors with borrowers, lending capital with interest while minimizing risk through rigorous verification of both applicants and vehicles offered as collateral.

The Problem

Business Challenge

The existing loan application funnel was hemorrhaging potential customers. Despite healthy traffic numbers, conversion rates from leads to approved loans remained below expectations. The company needed to understand why qualified applicants were abandoning the process.

Key Issues Identified:

  • High drop-off rates at specific funnel stages
  • Users couldn't complete applications in one session
  • Complex documentation requirements overwhelming applicants
  • Mobile users (75-80% of traffic) struggling with desktop-first design
75-80% of traffic came from mobile devices

Research & Discovery

I conducted comprehensive user research combining qualitative and quantitative methods to understand where and why users were dropping off.

1. Empathize Phase

Conducted contextual inquiry, user observation, and created detailed personas based on real user data.

Key Insights from Research

5-Second Test Failure

The landing page failed the 5-second test—users couldn't understand what we offered within critical first seconds.

Low Tech Literacy

Our target users weren't tech-savvy and struggled with complex digital processes.

Mobile-First Necessity

With 75-80% mobile traffic, the desktop-centric design was failing the majority of users.

Broken User Journey

A critical bifurcation forced users to leave the form, check email, and access a separate platform—causing massive drop-off.

Documentation Overload

Requiring 11 separate documents was overwhelming users and creating friction in the upload process.

Technical Jargon Barrier

Requirements like "technical file of the vehicle" confused users, leading to errors and abandonment.

2. Define Phase

Analyzed data through funnel analysis, information synthesis, and clusterization to identify pain points and opportunities.

Core Problem Statement

How might we simplify a complex financial application process for mobile-first, less tech-savvy users while maintaining necessary security and compliance requirements?

The Design Process

3. Ideate & Prototype

Conducted brainstorming sessions with stakeholders, created wireframes, and developed functional mockups in Figma.

Solution 1: Streamlined Form Flow

The Challenge: Users had to navigate 4 separate form screens, leaving the website to check email mid-process.

The Solution: Reduced the form from 4 screens to just 2, eliminating the email verification step and keeping users within a single, continuous flow.

Before: 4-screen fragmented flow with email interruption
After: 2-screen streamlined flow

Solution 2: Intelligent Documentation Consolidation

The Challenge: Requesting 11 separate documents was overwhelming users and causing abandonment.

The Solution: Used card sorting methodology to reorganize documents into 5 logical groups, combining related items (especially photos) to simplify the upload process.

From 11 to 5

Reduced cognitive load by 55% by grouping related documents

Photo Grouping

Combined vehicle photos into single uploads for efficiency

Progressive Disclosure

Only showed required fields at each step

Solution 3: Error Prevention Through Education

The Challenge: The "technical file" requirement confused users—it's a traffic institution document that's not commonly understood.

The Solution: Applied Nielsen's error prevention heuristic by creating detailed, platform-specific guidance:

  • Step-by-step visual instructions for Android and iPhone
  • Explained how to use native camera functions for high-resolution scanning
  • Placed explanatory images using proximity principle—right next to the document description

Solution 4: WhatsApp Integration

The Insight: Competitor analysis revealed mobile-first companies were leveraging familiar communication channels.

The Solution: Integrated WhatsApp for document uploads and real-time support:

  • Added CTA button with WhatsApp link for instant call center connection
  • Integrated with CRM for seamless handoff to support team
  • Offered alternative: direct upload or scheduled callback option

4. Test & Iterate

Conducted usability testing, heuristic evaluation, and analyzed heatmaps to validate solutions and identify edge cases.

Critical Discovery During Testing

Edge Case Identified: The vehicle detection API (license plate recognition) could fail, leaving users stuck.

Solution: Added fallback screen with manual vehicle identification form, ensuring users could always complete the process even when automation failed.

Qualitative Improvements

  • Mobile-first experience: Optimized for 75-80% of our actual user base
  • Reduced cognitive load: Simplified technical language and consolidated documentation
  • Error prevention: Step-by-step guidance reduced user mistakes
  • Instant support: WhatsApp integration enabled real-time assistance
  • Robust fallbacks: Manual entry option ensured process completion even with API failures

Key Learnings

This project was both exciting and complex, given the volume of documentation, process steps, edge cases, and information required for vehicle appraisal and loan qualification.

What I Learned

  • Simplification is strategic: In financial services, reducing friction while maintaining compliance requires careful balance and stakeholder buy-in
  • Mobile-first isn't optional: When 75%+ of users are mobile, desktop-first design actively harms conversion
  • Error prevention > error handling: Proactive education (like platform-specific scanning guides) prevents problems before they happen
  • Always plan for failure states: The API fallback was discovered late but proved critical—automated systems need manual alternatives
  • Meet users where they are: WhatsApp integration worked because it leveraged existing user behavior

Collaboration & Process

Worked closely with cross-functional teams including developers, marketing department, and CEO to balance user needs with business requirements and technical constraints. Regular testing and iteration based on real user feedback ensured we delivered a solution that worked in the real world, not just in theory.
