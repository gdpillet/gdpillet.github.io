CASE STUDY 02

North Star UX Strategy: Scaling Fintech Loan Conversion

Redefining a personal loan application flow to increase qualified conversion, reduce drop-off, and elevate the customer experience at a major fintech platform.

Role

Sr. Product Designer

Timeline

4-6 months

Cross-Functional Team

8-10 stakeholders

The Problem

Despite a best-in-class UI and brand, our personal loan application funnel was underperforming:

  • High Drop-off Rates: Many users started applications but did not complete them, particularly during verification steps.
  • Unclear Expectations: Users did not know how long the process would take or what information they'd need to provide, leading to frustration.
  • Low Trust Signal: The application didn't clearly communicate security, data privacy, or approval likelihood, creating hesitation.

Our stakeholders (product, leadership, and customer support) agreed: we needed a North Star UX strategy to reimagine our loan flow, balancing higher conversion with better user experience.

Business Impact

18%
increase in qualified loan application conversion rate
-22%
reduction in application drop-off during identity verification and final review steps
+35%
user satisfaction scores (post-apply NPS) improved significantly
+12%
increase in total completed loan applications submitted

Role & Context

I was brought into this initiative as the Senior Product Designer responsible for reimagining our entire loan application UX. This was the most high-priority and highest-visibility project for our Product and Business teams.

What I Owned: The full design lifecycle from research to high-fidelity UI, prototyping, and design QA. I was responsible for presenting design recommendations to leadership and running stakeholder design reviews.

Collaborators: I worked closely with our Head of Product (who owned the north-star vision), a Product Manager (for requirements and metrics), a Customer Insights Analyst (for quantitative and qualitative data), Engineering leads (for feasibility), Compliance and Legal (for regulatory constraints), and Customer Support (for feedback on pain points).

Design Process & Solution

Phase 1: Strategic Foundation (Metrics + UX)

I started by aligning with stakeholders on core metrics. We agreed our north star metric was "Qualified Conversion Rate", the % of users who successfully completed a loan application after starting one.

Secondary metrics included:

  • Drop-off rate at each stage (initiation, identity verification, final review)
  • Time-to-completion (average time users spent completing the flow)
  • Support ticket volume related to application confusion

Initial Research: I collaborated with our Customer Insights team to analyze existing behavioral data (Mixpanel, session recordings). I identified major drop-off points: identity verification (users confused by document upload process) and final loan review (unclear next steps after submission).

Phase 2: Strategic Competitive Benchmarking (+ UX)

I conducted a competitive analysis of top fintech loan applications (SoFi, LendingClub, Upstart, Marcus by Goldman Sachs). I documented:

  • How competitors structured their flows (single-page vs. multi-step)
  • How they set user expectations (progress indicators, time estimates)
  • How they built trust (security badges, privacy statements, approval likelihood)
  • How they handled document verification (automation vs. manual upload)

Key Insight: The best-performing fintechs used progressive disclosure, they only asked for information when it was contextually necessary, reducing cognitive overload. They also emphasized transparency (showing exactly what would happen next).

Phase 3: Design / Ideation (Core UX Workflows + UI)

Based on research insights, I designed 3 major improvements to our loan application flow:

1. Streamlined Multi-Step Flow with Clear Expectations

  • Redesigned the application as a clean, linear multi-step experience with a persistent progress bar showing % completion and estimated time remaining.
  • Introduced contextual help tooltips and inline validation (real-time feedback on fields like income, employment status).
  • Added micro-copy that explained why we needed each piece of information ("We ask for your employment info to verify income and ensure you qualify").

2. Improved Identity Verification & Document Upload

  • Simplified document upload by integrating an automated ID scanning tool (using a third-party API).
  • Designed a drag-and-drop upload interface with clear visual feedback (file preview, upload success/error states).
  • Added reassurance messaging around data security ("Your documents are encrypted and never shared").

3. Post-Submission Clarity & Next Steps

  • Redesigned the confirmation screen to clearly show: (1) What happens next, (2) When users would hear back, (3) How to track their application status.
  • Introduced email and SMS notifications at key milestones (application received, under review, approved/denied).
  • Created a "Track Your Application" dashboard where users could log back in and see real-time status updates.

Phase 4: Prototyping & User Testing

I created high-fidelity Figma prototypes and conducted moderated usability testing with 12 participants (mix of existing customers and prospects). Key findings:

  • Progress indicators significantly reduced anxiety ("I know exactly where I am and how much is left").
  • Inline validation prevented errors and reduced form abandonment.
  • Document upload clarity was a major improvement ("I finally understand what I need to submit").
  • Post-submission tracking was highly valued ("I don't feel left in the dark anymore").

Based on feedback, I refined the design to add more prominent CTAs and clearer error messaging.

Phase 5: Handoff to Engineering & A/B Test Measurement

I worked closely with engineering to ensure pixel-perfect implementation and smooth handoff. We launched the new flow as an A/B test (50/50 split between old and new experience).

Results after 2 months:

  • 18% increase in qualified conversion rate
  • 22% reduction in drop-off during verification
  • 35% improvement in post-application NPS
  • 12% increase in total applications submitted

The new flow became the default experience and was adopted company-wide.

Trade-offs & Constraints

Balancing Simplicity with Legal/Compliance Requirements

Challenge: Legal and Compliance required extensive disclosures and consent forms throughout the application. This added friction and increased cognitive load.

Trade-off: I worked with Legal to identify which disclosures were legally required vs. "nice to have." We moved non-critical disclosures to collapsible sections and summarized key points in plain language.

Resolution: We created a "progressive disclosure" model where users could expand sections if they wanted more details, but the core flow remained clean and scannable. This satisfied Legal while improving UX.

Technical Limitations with ID Verification

Challenge: Our initial plan was to integrate a fully automated ID verification API (like Persona or Onfido). However, engineering flagged significant integration challenges and cost concerns.

Trade-off: We had to compromise on a semi-automated solution: users could still upload documents manually, but we added smarter validation (e.g., detecting blurry images, flagging missing info).

Resolution: This reduced some of the UX smoothness I envisioned, but we still saw a 22% reduction in verification drop-off. We documented the fully automated solution as a future enhancement.

Adoption Resistance from Internal Teams

Challenge: Customer Support was initially resistant to the new flow, worried it would create confusion and increase support tickets during the transition.

Approach: I organized training sessions and created detailed documentation (including annotated screenshots and FAQs) to help Support understand the new experience and how to guide customers through it.

Resolution: Post-launch, support tickets actually decreased by 15% because the new flow was more intuitive. Support became advocates for the redesign.

Collaboration & Conflict Resolution

Aligning Sales & Engineering Priorities

Challenge: Sales wanted aggressive conversion optimization (e.g., removing friction points, simplifying the application to the bare minimum). Engineering wanted to minimize scope and ship quickly. These priorities were at odds.

Approach: I facilitated a prioritization workshop where we mapped out the user journey and identified which improvements would have the highest impact with the lowest engineering lift. We used a "high-impact, low-effort" matrix to align on priorities.

Resolution: We agreed on a phased rollout: Phase 1 focused on quick wins (progress indicators, inline validation), Phase 2 on more complex features (automated document upload, application tracking dashboard). This satisfied both teams.

Convincing a Skeptical Head of Product

Challenge: Our Head of Product was initially skeptical about investing in a "North Star" redesign, worried it would delay other priorities and might not deliver ROI.

Approach: I presented a data-driven case: competitive benchmarks showing how competitors with better loan UX had higher conversion rates, user research highlighting major pain points, and a projected ROI model based on expected conversion lift.

Resolution: The Head of Product became a strong advocate for the project and helped secure executive buy-in. The 18% conversion lift validated the investment.

Dealing with Mid-Project Requirement Changes

Challenge: Midway through design, Compliance introduced new regulatory requirements (additional disclosures for certain loan types). This threatened to derail our simplified flow.

Approach: I quickly iterated on the design to accommodate the new requirements without sacrificing UX. I proposed conditional logic: only showing additional disclosures for loan types that required them, rather than adding them universally.

Resolution: Engineering implemented the conditional logic, and we maintained the clean experience for most users while staying compliant.

Impact & Reflection

18% Conversion Lift

The new loan application flow drove an 18% increase in qualified conversion, translating to millions in additional loan volume.

-22% Drop-off Reduction

Identity verification and final review steps saw a 22% reduction in abandonment, significantly improving completion rates.

+35% NPS Improvement

Post-application user satisfaction scores improved by 35%, with users praising the clarity and ease of the new flow.

-15% Support Tickets

Customer support tickets related to loan applications decreased by 15%, reducing operational burden.

"This redesign transformed our loan application experience. Our customers tell us it's now the easiest loan application they've ever completed, and the business impact speaks for itself." , Head of Product

Learnings

Trust and transparency are conversion drivers. Users need to feel confident in the process. Clear expectations, progress indicators, and security messaging were as important as visual polish.

Progressive disclosure reduces cognitive overload. By only asking for information when it was contextually relevant, we kept users engaged and reduced abandonment.

Data-driven design builds stakeholder trust. Presenting competitive benchmarks, user research, and projected ROI helped secure buy-in from skeptical stakeholders and ensured alignment throughout the project.

Cross-functional collaboration is critical. Working closely with Legal, Compliance, Engineering, and Support ensured the final design was both user-friendly and technically/legally feasible. Facilitating alignment workshops and maintaining open communication prevented conflicts and kept the project on track.

Iteration and testing are non-negotiable. User testing revealed critical insights that shaped the final design. Without validating assumptions with real users, we would have missed key opportunities to improve the experience.
